My Research

Physics-informed Machine Learning

My work explores novel ways to integrate physical laws directly into machine learning models, leading to more accurate, stable, and generalizable solutions for complex systems. This is crucial for applications where data is scarce or expensive.

  • Multi-fidelity Physics-informed Generative Adversarial Networks (MF-PIGANs) for PDEs: Developed a pioneering approach that combines the strengths of PINNs, GANs, and multi-fidelity modeling to solve partial differential equations. This includes physics-supervision in the adversarial process, mode collapse mitigation, and first-order PDE formulation for improved accuracy and stability. (Paper P4)
  • Interpretable Physics-informed Graph Neural Networks (HydroGraphNet) for Flood Forecasting: Introduced a novel framework integrating Kolmogorov-Arnold Network (KAN) to enhance model interpretability and embedding mass conservation laws for physically consistent predictions in unstructured mesh-based flood forecasting. (Paper P1)

Graph Neural Networks for Infrastructure Systems

Leveraging GNNs to model complex spatial and topological dependencies in critical infrastructure, enhancing predictive capabilities for real-world phenomena like flood dynamics and power flow.

  • Multi-fidelity GNNs for Flood Hazard Mapping: Proposed a hierarchical learning framework unifying coarse- and fine-resolution data for efficient and accurate flood hazard mapping (both pluvial and fluvial flooding). This approach significantly outperforms single-fidelity models, demonstrating up to a 60% reduction in Mean Absolute Error (MAE) and enabling more efficient real-time flood forecasting for disaster preparedness. (Paper U1)
  • Multi-fidelity GNNs for Power Flow Analysis: Developed a multi-fidelity GNN-based surrogate model for efficient probabilistic power flow analysis under high-dimensional demand and renewable generation uncertainty. This model significantly reduces the computational burden of probabilistic power flow calculations and exhibits enhanced robustness to minor topology changes and promising performance on unseen grid topologies, crucial for modernizing power systems. (Paper P3)
  • Multifidelity GNNs for Mesh-based PDE Surrogate Modeling: Pioneered MFGNN frameworks for efficient and accurate mesh-based Partial Differential Equation (PDE) surrogate modeling across various computational sciences. By combining low-fidelity and high-fidelity data, this work significantly reduces training computational costs while improving the accuracy, robustness, and generalizability of solutions for complex systems governed by PDEs. (Paper P2)
  • NVIDIA PhysicsNeMo Contribution: Integrated the HydroGraphNet model into NVIDIA's PhysicsNeMo framework, demonstrating practical software engineering skills and collaboration in a leading tech environment. (NVIDIA PhysicsNeMo Pull Request)

Neural Operators & Domain Adaptation

Exploring advanced neural network architectures that learn mappings between infinite-dimensional function spaces, coupled with strategies to improve model generalizability across diverse data distributions.

  • Domain-Adaptive Geometry-Informed Neural Operators (GINO) for Rapid Flood Forecasting: Introduced Flood Forecaster, a novel framework integrating GNOs and FNOs through a three-stage architecture (Geometry Encoder, Global Model, Geometry Decoder) for high-resolution flood inundation mapping. Incorporates Gradient Reversal Layer (GRL) for domain adaptation, promoting learning of domain-invariant features and enhancing generalizability in data-scarce scenarios. (Paper I2)

Uncertainty Quantification & Computational Efficiency

Developing robust and efficient methods for quantifying uncertainty in complex systems and optimizing computational processes, crucial for reliable AI/ML predictions.

  • Improving Accuracy and Computational Efficiency of Optimal Design of Experiments via Greedy Backward Approach: Proposed a novel greedy algorithm that significantly enhances the accuracy, robustness, and computational efficiency of optimal Design of Experiments (DoEs) for Polynomial Chaos Expansion (PCE), especially in high-dimensional problems. (Paper P5)
  • Probabilistic Framework for Evaluating Seismic Resilience of Transportation Systems: Developed a probabilistic framework for evaluating seismic resilience during emergency medical response, integrating hazard, risk, network, and agent-based models. While not directly ML, this project demonstrates strong quantitative modeling, systems thinking, and data-driven approaches. (Paper P6)
