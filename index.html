Research & Selected Projects

Problem–driven work across floods, power systems, and large-scale transportation data.

Multi-fidelity GNNs for Flood Hazard Mapping

Efficient, high-resolution flood surrogates on unstructured meshes

Traditional hydrodynamic models are too slow for large-scale, high-resolution hazard mapping. I developed a multi-fidelity graph neural network (MFGNN) framework that combines coarse and fine-resolution simulations on unstructured meshes for rapid, accurate flood predictions.

  • Problem: Generate detailed flood maps over large domains without prohibitive compute costs.
  • Approach: Two-stage GNN pipeline where a low-fidelity model learns broad dynamics and a second GNN learns a high-resolution residual correction.
  • Impact: Achieved substantial error reductions versus single-fidelity baselines and enabled scalable hazard mapping suitable for near real-time forecasting and scenario analysis.
Geometry-Informed Neural Operators (GINO)

Domain-adaptive operator learning for dynamic flood forecasting

To move beyond static hazard maps, I developed FloodForecaster, a time-dependent geometry-informed neural operator that learns spatiotemporal flood dynamics and adapts across geographic domains.

  • Problem: Forecast floods in new regions with limited local data while retaining knowledge from data-rich basins.
  • Approach: Hybrid GNN/Fourier operator with domain adaptation via a gradient reversal layer, regularizing feature representations across source and target domains.
  • Impact: Delivered >100× speed-ups over hydrodynamic solvers and large reductions in target-domain RMSE compared with conventional fine-tuning, while preserving physical stability.
HydroGraphNet & PhysicsNeMo

Physics-informed GNNs with interpretable KAN layers

I created HydroGraphNet, a physics-informed GNN for flood forecasting that embeds mass conservation directly into the loss function and uses Kolmogorov–Arnold Networks (KANs) for interpretability.

  • Problem: Improve trust and physical consistency in ML-based flood forecasts on complex meshes.
  • Approach: Mass-conserving message-passing architecture with KAN-based node encoders to expose learned relationships between inputs and outputs.
  • Impact: Achieved large error reductions and near-zero mass balance error; integrated the model into NVIDIA PhysicsNeMo with unit tests, configs, and documentation, demonstrating production-quality engineering.
Power Systems & Physical Networks

Multi-fidelity GNN surrogates for probabilistic power flow

For modern grids with high renewable penetration, I built GNN-based surrogates for probabilistic power flow analysis under high-dimensional demand and generation uncertainty.

  • Problem: Repeated probabilistic power flow runs are too slow for real-time planning and risk analysis.
  • Approach: Multi-fidelity GNNs that learn from ensembles of low-cost simulations and a limited set of high-fidelity runs, while being robust to minor topology changes.
  • Impact: Reduced the computational burden of probabilistic studies and showed promising generalization to unseen grid configurations.
Connected Vehicle Data & Safety

Big-data analytics for proactive multimodal safety

As Lead Analyst on a Virginia DOT project, I designed and operated a full pipeline for 15.6B+ connected-vehicle events to quantify speeding and harsh events and relate them to infrastructure features such as bike lanes and crosswalks.

  • Problem: Traditional crash data are sparse and reactive, limiting proactive safety management.
  • Approach: Distributed data processing (Spark, Python, geospatial tools) to derive surrogate safety metrics and spatially join them with roadway attributes.
  • Impact: Produced detailed safety diagnostics and recommendations for targeted infrastructure improvements and enforcement strategies.
Generative Models & Optimization

Graph-based diffusion for constrained trajectory optimization

In ongoing postdoctoral work, I am exploring graph-based diffusion models coupled with differentiable optimization layers to tackle constrained trajectory and decision problems in complex networks.

  • Problem: Find feasible, high-quality control sequences under strict physical and operational constraints.
  • Approach: Hybrid discrete–continuous diffusion with a physics/optimization-informed projection layer that enforces feasibility while steering toward low-cost solutions.
  • Relevance: The same ideas extend naturally to robotics, manufacturing, and other cyber-physical systems where we must balance model expressiveness with reliability.
