About Me

Mehdi Taghizadeh, Ph.D.

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the University of Virginia, where I am continuing my passion for pushing the boundaries of Artificial Intelligence to solve complex scientific and engineering problems. I recently completed my Ph.D. in Civil Engineering in August 2025, building upon my B.Sc. and M.Sc. degrees from Sharif University of Technology where I consistently achieved top academic honors.

My doctoral research focused on developing cutting-edge AI frameworks for modeling real-world phenomena, culminating in my dissertation, "Advances in Graph-Based Learning for Flood Forecasting." My work has produced novel architectures like Multi-Fidelity GNNs, interpretable Physics-informed GNNs (HydroGraphNet), and Domain-Adaptive Neural Operators (GINO) that significantly advance the speed, accuracy, and generalizability of physical system simulations.

Currently, as a Postdoc with Prof. Ferdinando (Nando) Fioretto, I am architecting a generative AI framework to tackle the large-scale, computationally intractable power system restoration problem. This research involves developing a graph diffusion model to generate optimal sequences of control actions, framing the challenge as a complex trajectory optimization task.

I am actively seeking full-time AI/ML Research Scientist positions where I can apply my expertise in GNNs, generative models, and physics-informed learning to contribute to groundbreaking innovations and solve meaningful, real-world challenges.

My Research

Generative AI for Power System Restoration (Postdoc)

Architecting a generative AI framework to solve the large-scale power system restoration problem, framed as a trajectory optimization task on complex AC networks (a large-scale MINLP). This involves developing a Graph Diffusion model to generate optimal sequences of control actions for transitioning grids from blacked-out to secure states.

Physics-informed Machine Learning

My work explores novel ways to integrate physical laws directly into machine learning models, leading to more accurate, stable, and generalizable solutions for complex systems. This is crucial for applications where data is scarce or expensive.

  • Multi-fidelity Physics-informed Generative Adversarial Networks (MF-PIGANs) for PDEs: Developed a pioneering approach that combines the strengths of PINNs, GANs, and multi-fidelity modeling to solve partial differential equations. (Paper P5)
  • Interpretable Physics-informed Graph Neural Networks (HydroGraphNet) for Flood Forecasting: Introduced a novel framework integrating Kolmogorov-Arnold Network (KAN) to enhance model interpretability and embedding mass conservation laws for physically consistent predictions in unstructured mesh-based flood forecasting. (Paper P2)

Graph Neural Networks for Infrastructure Systems

Leveraging GNNs to model complex spatial and topological dependencies in critical infrastructure, enhancing predictive capabilities for real-world phenomena like flood dynamics and power flow.

  • Multi-fidelity GNNs for Flood Hazard Mapping: Proposed a hierarchical learning framework unifying coarse- and fine-resolution data for efficient and accurate flood hazard mapping (both pluvial and fluvial flooding), achieving up to a 60% reduction in Mean Absolute Error (MAE). (Paper P1)
  • NVIDIA PhysicsNeMo Contribution: Integrated the HydroGraphNet model into NVIDIA's PhysicsNeMo framework, demonstrating practical software engineering skills. (Pull Request)

Neural Operators & Domain Adaptation

Exploring advanced neural network architectures that learn mappings between infinite-dimensional function spaces, coupled with strategies to improve model generalizability across diverse data distributions.

  • Domain-Adaptive Geometry-Informed Neural Operators (GINO) for Rapid Flood Forecasting: Introduced Flood Forecaster, a novel framework integrating GNOs and FNOs. Incorporates a Gradient Reversal Layer (GRL) for domain adaptation, enhancing generalizability in data-scarce scenarios. (Paper U1)
